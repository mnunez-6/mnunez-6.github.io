Project Dataset¶

This dataset was obtained from the public Kaggle dataset Data Science Salaries 2023. The dataset contains a variety of factors regarding Data Science positions, including details about the year of work, experience levels, types of employment, salaries in local currencies and USD, remote work ratios, and other useful factors. This dataset provides a source of information that allows us to analyze trends in salaries, compare job titles, investigate geographical differences, measure the impact of remote work, and examine the correlation between the company size and salaries within the field of Data Science.

Dataset Columns:¶

work_year: The year the work and salary payment took place.

experience_level: The level of the employee's experience. (e.g., EN - Entry-level, MI - Mid-level, SE - Senior, EX - Executive)

employment_type: The category of employment. (e.g., FT - Full-time, PT - Part-time, CT - Contract, FL - Freelance)

job_title: The title of the employee's position (e.g., Data Scientist, Data Analyst, etc.)

salary: The salary as reported in the local currency.

salary_currency: The currency in which the salary was reported.

salary_in_usd: The equivalent salary in USD.

employee_residence: The employee's country of residence.

remote_ratio: The proportion of work done remotely. (0 - on-site, 50 - hybrid, 100 - completely remote)

company_location: The geographic location of the company.

company_size: The company's size classification. (S - Small, M - Medium, L - Large)

Project Goals¶

The goal of this project is to investigate how salaries differ across various expereince levels, job titles, employment types, and geographical locations. Additonally, we will examine the impact of remote work on salaries and how company size influences compensation. Through the analysis of this dataset, we hope to provide insights into the financial landscape of data science in the modern world, allowing both employers and employees to understand the key factors that drive salary differences in the field of Data Science.

Research Questions¶

The questions that I will be adressing are:

  1. What is the distribution of data science salaries based on various expereince levels, and how do these salaries vary across different job titles?
  2. How does the remote work ratio impact data science salaries and what are the differences in salary between remote, hybrid, and on-site positions.
  3. What are the geographical trends in data science salaries, and how do salaries differ based on the employee's residence and company location?
  4. How does company size affect data science salaries, and are there significant differences in compensation between small, medium, and large companies.

ETL (Extraction, Transform, Load)¶

We loaded the dataset Data Science Salaries in 2023 from Kaggle. This dataset displays the year of work, employee experience level, position title, the salary that was paid in local currency and USD, the country the employee resides in, the remote ratio of the position, and the location and size of the company.

The original dataset contained two additional columns, 'salary' and 'salay_currency'. These columns included salaries in various currencies, making direct comparisons problematic. However, the dataset also includes a 'salary_in_usd' column, which standarizes salaries by converting them all to USD, allowing for consisitent and accurate comparisons. Therefore, we cleaned the dataset by dropping the 'salary' and 'salary_currency' columns, focusing primarily on the 'salary_in_usd' column. Additionally, we reordered the columns of the dataset to group relation information together.
